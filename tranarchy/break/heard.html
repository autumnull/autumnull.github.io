^ back to break: a hypertext exercise in hyperempathy

vox popularis

“i remember when my voice first started dropping – if you can believe i was ever less femme than this” swishing her hair – “but it was like, just at the end of primary school, i think mine dropped a little earlier and a little deeper than most, and like… i met up with my primary school friends after not seeing them for a year and they were all like “holy shit your voice??” and sorta fawned about it. it was a pretty odd experience that felt a lot like being a lion cub growing its mane, like, scruffy and adolescently impressive and kind of unwieldy alongside the rest of your body. that last part might just be dysphoria, idk.”

“probably, yeah. i do feel the lion cub thing tho, the character of arrogance and being a wild little creature who’s skulking on the fringes of maleness. especially because i’m going through this deliberately so it’s the, the transmasc thing of appropriating masculinity like, uh…”

“prometheus?”
