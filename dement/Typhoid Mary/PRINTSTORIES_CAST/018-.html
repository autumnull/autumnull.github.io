TYPHOID MARY tattoo 4

Typhoid Mary prints out some lines she has drawn at Sister Antonia's. She takes them to the tattooist and shows them to him. He says it will cost $80 to put the lines on Typhoid Mary's back. He says it will look good. That's an unusual thing for him to say. He is a mean bastard.
Typhoid Mary goes in. She takes off her singlet and lowers her fishnet lingerie. The tattooist draws the design on her back and shows her in a mirror.
"That's it." says Typhoid Mary.
The tattooist sprays antiseptic on her back and starts to tattoo. Typhoid Mary breathes and closes her eyes. The pain takes away the hatred. The pain takes away the burden and the torture. There is just the tattooing and the tattooist. It feels like love. It feels like truth and knowledge. There is no room for turmoil or doubt. The pain fills the hole. It is enough. Typhoid Mary has peace.
"Here and now in this chair with my tattooist all is right with my world." says Typhoid Mary.
She looks at the dirty lino near the fridge. The fridge has Harley Davidson stickers on it and a picture drawn by the tattooist's daughter. There is an extension cord and a mask with horns. American heavy rock music is playing. There is peace. Peace and love. The tattooist gets up and takes a coke from the fridge. He goes back to tattooing. Sometimes he sits back and drinks the coke and looks at Typhoid Mary's back.
 
When it is finished the tattooist holds up the mirror so Typhoid Mary can see the new tattoo. "It's beautiful." she says. She can't see a thing. She is too much at peace to be able to see.
