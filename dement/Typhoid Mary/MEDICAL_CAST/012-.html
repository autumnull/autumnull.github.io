"The onset is generally insidious with lethargy, malaise, frontal headache, muscular aching and joint pains. A dry cough is common. The temperature rises in step-ladder fashion reaching its acme at the end of a week. The pyrexia continues unabated throughout the second and third weeks. When the outcome is favourable the temperature falls slowly by lysis, returning to normal in the fourth week."
