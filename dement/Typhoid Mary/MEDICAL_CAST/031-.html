The Eighth or Vestiulocochlear Nerve

Both the internal ear and the eighth nerve which supplies it have two functions: hearing and equilibrium. The cochlea, concerned with hearing, is supplied by the part of the eighth nerve known as the cochlear part, while the semi circular canals, the utricle and the saccule, which are concerned with the recognition of the position of the head in realtion to gravity and its movement in space, are supplied by that portion of the eighth nerve known as the vestibular part.
