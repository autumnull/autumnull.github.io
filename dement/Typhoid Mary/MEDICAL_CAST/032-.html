The Vestibular Fibres and the Functions of the Labyrinth

The part of the labyrinth concerned with equilibrium consists of the semi circular canals, the utricle, and the saccule. The semi circular canals are hollows in the petrous part of the temporal bone, the osseous canals being occupied by membranous tubes filled with endolymph and separated from the bony walls by perilymph.
