"The typical rash of typhoid fever may appear towards the end of the first week but its appearance has been recorded as late as the 20th day. It is present in approximately 50% of adults with typhoid but is seen less frequently in children. Rose spots are difficult to detect on dark skins.
The rash is distributed over the abdomen and chest and may extend on to the back and proximal parts of the limbs but is rarely seen on the face, hands or feet." p79
